  5. Aug 2024 - Jan 2025

    Image-Colouration

    This project tackles the challenging problem of automatic image colorization, leveraging deep learning architectures to transform grayscale images into realistic color versions for applications in image restoration, enhancement, and creative tasks. By experimenting with progressively advanced models—starting with a simple CNN-based encoder-decoder, then a U-Net with a pre-trained ResNet backbone, and finally a conditional GAN with a U-Net generator—the project demonstrates improvements in color fidelity and realism. Key metrics like Mean Absolute Error (MAE) improved from 0.0848 to 0.0807, and Learned Perceptual Image Patch Similarity (LPIPS) scores (using VGG) improved from 0.148 to 0.132, showcasing the effectiveness of GANs in generating context-aware, high-quality colorized images.
